Intrinsic decoherence in the three-mode coupled harmonic oscillators

April 4, 2021
idea open problem classical quantum

Idea: A three-mode coupled harmonic oscillators have a Hamiltonian in the form

$$(1)\qquad\hat{H} = \frac{1}{2}\sum\limits_{i = 1}^{3}\hat{p}_{k}^{2} + \sum\limits_{j<k}^{3}g_{j,k}\hspace{5px} \hat{q}_{j}\hat{q}_{k},$$

where the coupling strengths \(g_{j,k}\) are time-independent.

Now, there exist a framework1 for ripping off the non-diagonal terms in (1) by performing an angle-dependent unitary transformation \(\hat{T}(\theta_{j,k})\). Given that last, we can address the problem to calculate the intrinsic decoherence of the system for the master equation

$$(2)\qquad \dot{\rho} = -\mathrm{i}\left[\hat{H},\rho\right] + 2\gamma\left(2\hat{H}\rho\hat{H} - \hat{H}^{2}\rho - \rho\hat{H}^{2}\right),$$

for some density matrix \(\rho\)? —Maybe a so called coherent state \( \ket{\alpha}\).

Supposition: If the last is true, we can arrive to a description of the energy transport in the three-mode coupled harmonic oscillators assisted by the decoherence of the time evolution given by (2).

The literature give us an example in the atom-field interaction2, and a practical realization in RLC circuits3.
