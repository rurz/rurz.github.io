Finding classical deaths and revivals, like in the atom-field interaction?

March 27, 2021
classical quantum atom-field analogies

Maybe it’s a tricky question to put a link between the diametrical phenomenology of classical and quantum mechanics; but, for the most of the history, we use the first as an archetype to extend the comprehension of the second. Working around a superb-classical system like a set of \(N\) coupled harmonic oscillators

$$\ddot{q_{i}}(t) = -(k_{i} + k_{i+1})q_{i}(t) + k_{i+1}q_{i+1}(t) + k_{i}q_{i-1}(t),$$

we can solve the set of equation with a plethora of methods developed in the last centuries. The last year, I took a look on this problem at 1, nowadays I was trying to find the dynamical energy exchange between oscillators, just to arrive a peculiar phenomena of the individual amplitude evolution of \(q_{n}(t)\), who in turn seems to be the mimic of the atom-field interaction in quantum mechanics, better known as the Jaynnes-Cummings system

$$\hat{H} = \omega_{f} \hat{a}\hat{a}^{\dagger} + \omega_{a} \hat{\sigma}_{z} + \Omega\left(\hat{a}\hat{\sigma}_{+} + \hat{a}^{\dagger}\hat{\sigma}_{-}\right)$$

  1. Urzúa, A. R., Ramos-Prieto, I., Soto-Eguibar, F., & Moya-Cessa, H. (2020). Dynamical analysis of mass–spring models using Lie algebraic methods. Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and Its Applications, 540, 123193. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physa.2019.123193 ↩︎
