Finding classical deaths and revivals, like in the atom-field interaction?

April 3, 2021
idea open problem classical quantum analogies

Maybe it’s a tricky task to put a link between the diametrical phenomenology of classical and quantum mechanics; but, for the most of the history, we use the first as an archetype to extend the comprehension of the second. Working around a superb-classical system, like a set of \(N\) coupled harmonic oscillators

$$(1)\qquad \ddot{q_{i}}(t) = -(k_{i} + k_{i+1})q_{i}(t) + k_{i+1}q_{i+1}(t) + k_{i}q_{i-1}(t),$$

we can solve the set of equation with a plethora of methods developed in the last centuries. The last year, I took a look on this problem at 1, nowadays I was trying to find the dynamical energy exchange between oscillators, just to arrive a peculiar phenomena of the individual amplitude evolution of \(q_{n}(t)\), who in turn seems to be the mimic of the atom-field interaction in quantum mechanics, better known as the Jaynnes-Cummings system

$$(2)\quad \hat{H} = \omega_{f} \hat{a}\hat{a}^{\dagger} + \omega_{a} \hat{\sigma}_{z} + \Omega\left(\hat{a}\hat{\sigma}_{+} + \hat{a}^{\dagger}\hat{\sigma}_{-}\right).$$

Of course, the analogy is established when a particular realization of the set of equations (1) is given. The last fulfills when the coefficients \(k\)’s belongs to the elements of a certain representation of the \(su(2)\) algebra; in particular, we arrive to the eigenfunctions of the discrete and finite harmonic oscillator, namely Kravchuk functions.

To put on the fast-lane, we have

  1. The solution of (1) with a set of initial conditions at positions \(\require{braket} \ket{0} = (1, 0, \cdots, 0)^{T}\). The initial conditions at velocities are zero, and the coupling coefficients are depicted as in 1.

  1. The solution of (2) under the evolution of the Schrödinger equation with the initial state as a coherent function in Fock space \(\require{braket} \psi_{0} = \ket{\alpha = 5\mathrm{i}}\)

At this very initial state, I can only relate the envelopment of the figures, but in some deep analysis I (maybe) can relate the mathematical structure and physical insights of the two systems, who in turn, represent different phenomena, regardless their similitude in their patterns.

More work to do…
